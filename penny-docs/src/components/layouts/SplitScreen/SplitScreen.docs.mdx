## Usage

- Use Split Screen to **pair two related areas** side by side — most commonly a **job with an asset** (e.g. an invoice on the left, actions on the right).
- Each panel is **independently scrollable**, so a long form in one panel won't push the other off screen.
- Provide content to `panelA` and `panelB`. On large screens panel A is the **left** panel; on small screens it becomes the **top** panel.
- Optional layout-level `header` and `footer` span the **full width** above and below both panels.

## Variants

Three ratios control how the horizontal space is divided between the two panels.

- **`1:1`** — equal panels. Best when both sides carry comparable weight (e.g. share-invoice).
- **`1:2`** — narrow left, wide right (the default). Use when the right panel holds the primary workspace.
- **`2:1`** — wide left, narrow right. Use when the left panel is the focus and the right is supporting.

<Canvas of={SplitScreenStories.Variants} />

## Container width

Each panel has its own container, so each side can constrain its content to its own max-width — keep forms readable on the workspace side while letting a preview fill its panel. Panel A also accepts `minWidth` and `maxWidth` as percentage numbers to bound how far it can shrink or grow.

## Common use

The most common use is pairing a job with an asset — for example a **share-invoice** screen, where the invoice appears on the left and the actions on the right.

<Canvas of={SplitScreenStories.CommonUse} />

## Loading state

Set `isLoading` on the whole layout to replace both panels with a single centered Loader. Each panel also accepts its own `isLoading` to load independently.

<Canvas of={SplitScreenStories.LoadingState} />

## Mobile usage

On mobile, the two panels are rearranged into a **vertical stack** — panel A on top, panel B below, separated by a divider. The horizontal ratio no longer applies.

<Canvas of={SplitScreenStories.MobileStacking} />

<Callout variant="note">The switch between the side-by-side and stacked layouts happens automatically at the `l` (1240px) breakpoint — you don't need to pass anything to trigger it.</Callout>
